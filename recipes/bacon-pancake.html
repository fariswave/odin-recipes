Bacon Pancake

Description

Bacon Pancake is a breakfast dish blending crispy bacon with fluffy pancakes, creating a sweet-savory delight. Cooked by incorporating bacon strips into the pancake batter, it offers a unique flavor combination. Often served with maple syrup, it's a popular and indulgent breakfast choice.

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces center-cut thick-cut sliced bacon (about 12 slices)
  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 4 tablespoons (1/2 stick) unsalted butter, plus more for the pan
  • Maple syrup, for serving

Steps

  1. Working in batches if needed, place 12 ounces bacon in a single layer on a griddle or in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Cook, flipping occasionally, until just crisp, 8 to 10 minutes. Transfer the bacon to a paper towel-lined plate. Wipe the griddle or skillet clean with paper towels (pour off the grease and reserve before wiping if you want to use it to cook the pancakes).
  2. Place 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour, 1 tablespoon granulated sugar, 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder, 1 teaspoon baking soda, and 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt in a large bowl and whisk to combine.
  3. Place 2 cups buttermilk and 2 large eggs in a medium bowl and whisk to combine. Melt 4 tablespoons unsalted butter in the microwave or on the stovetop. Add the buttermilk mixture to the flour mixture, then add the melted butter. Stir until just combined — the batter should lumpy. Let rest 5 minutes. Meanwhile, heat the griddle or skillet over medium heat.
  4. Coat the pan with a thin layer of butter or bacon grease. Place 1 strip of the bacon in the pan and pour 1/4 cup batter over the center of the strip, allowing it to spread out and form a circle about 4 inches wide. Repeat with more strips of bacon and batter to fill the space on the griddle or skillet. (In a 12-inch skillet, you should be able to fit about 5 pancakes.) Cook until the bubbles form on the top and the bottoms are golden-brown, 3 to 4 minutes, adjusting the heat as needed. Flip the pancakes and cook until the second side is golden-brown, 2 to 3 minutes more.
  5. Transfer to plates or a 200ºF oven to keep warm. Repeat cooking the remaining strips of bacon and batter. Serve drizzled with maple syrup.
